Let $G$ be a group. Let $H \leq G$. Let $K \trianglelefteq G$. Then $H \cap K \trianglelefteq H$.

Proof: 
\begin{align*}
&\text{Let } h \in H, k \in H \cap K. \text{ Then } hkh^{-1} \in K \text{ since } K \trianglelefteq G. \\
&\text{Also } hkh^{-1} \in H \text{ since } h,k \in H. \\
&\text{So } hkh^{-1} \in H \cap K. \\
&\text{Therefore } H \cap K \trianglelefteq H.
\end{align*}

Now consider $HK = \{hk \mid h \in H, k \in K\}$.

Since $K \trianglelefteq G$, we have:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $HK = KH$
    \item $HK \leq G$
    \item $K \trianglelefteq HK$
    \item $H \cap K \trianglelefteq H$
\end{itemize}

Then by the Second Isomorphism Theorem:
\[
H/(H \cap K) \cong HK/K
\]
