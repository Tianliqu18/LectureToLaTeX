Let $G$ be a group, $H \leq G$, and $K \trianglelefteq G$. Let $\phi: G \to G/K$ be the natural homomorphism.

Then:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $\phi(H) = HK/K$
    \item $H \cap K \trianglelefteq H$
    \item $H/(H \cap K) \cong HK/K$
\end{enumerate}

Proof:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Since $K \trianglelefteq G$, we have $kK = K$ for all $k \in K$
    \item For $h \in H$, $\phi(h) = hK$
    \item Thus $\phi(H) = \{hK : h \in H\} = HK/K$
    \item $H \cap K \trianglelefteq H$ because $K \trianglelefteq G$
    \item By the First Isomorphism Theorem, $H/(H \cap K) \cong HK/K$
\end{itemize}
