\section*{Matrix Differential Equation Solution}

Consider the matrix function \(\Phi(t)\) given by:

\[
\Phi = \begin{bmatrix}
e^{-2t} & e^{-5t} \\
e^{-t} & 2e^{-5t}
\end{bmatrix}
\]

The determinant of \(\Phi\) is computed as:

\[
\det(\Phi) = 2e^{-6t}
\]

\section*{Inverse of the Matrix}

The inverse of the matrix \(\Phi\), denoted \(\Phi^{-1}\), can be calculated using the formula for the inverse of a 2x2 matrix:

\[
\Phi^{-1} = \frac{1}{3} \begin{bmatrix}
2e^{2t} & -e^{-5t} \\
-e^{-t} & e^{2t}
\end{bmatrix}
\]

\section*{Matrix \textit{U} and Particular Solution}

With \(\Phi^{-1}\), we compute the matrix \(\textit{U}\) as the integral of \(\Phi^{-1}\):

\[
\textit{U} = \int \begin{bmatrix}
\frac{1}{3} e^{2t}
\end{bmatrix}
= \begin{bmatrix}
\frac{100}{6} e^{2t} \\
\frac{100}{15} e^{50t}
\end{bmatrix}
\]

The particular solution \(\chi_p\) is then given by:

\[
\chi_p = \Phi \cdot \textit{U} = \begin{bmatrix}
e^{-2t} & e^{-5t} \\
e^{-t} & 2e^{-5t}
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
\frac{50}{3} e^{\frac{5}{2}t} \\
2e^{6t}
\end{bmatrix}
\]

\section*{Evaluating Matrix Products}

Calculations lead to:

\[
\begin{bmatrix}
\frac{50}{3} = \frac{20}{3} \\
50
\end{bmatrix}
+ 
\begin{bmatrix}
\frac{40}{3}
\end{bmatrix}
= 
\begin{bmatrix}
\frac{30}{3} \\
90
\end{bmatrix}
\]

Consequently, the particular solution is:

\[
\chi_p = \begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ 30 \end{bmatrix}
\]

This concludes the calculations for solving the given differential equation using matrix methods.
